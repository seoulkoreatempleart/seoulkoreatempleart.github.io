Scripture Study Reflections

Thoughts on Christian discipleship as a covenant daughter of God.

"But the fruit of the Spirit is...temperance..." Galatians 5:22-23
"I would that ye should be humble, and be submissive and gentle; easy to be entreated; full of patience and long-suffering; being temperate in all things." Alma 7:23

From Kent D. Watson: [T]emperance is a divine attribute of Jesus Christ...Learning to be temperate in all things is a spiritual gift available through the Holy Ghost.

Tempered glass, like tempered steel, undergoes a well-controlled heating process which increases strength...

Likewise, a temperate soul-one who is humble and full of love-is also a person of increased spiritual strength...

Security for our families comes from learning self-control, avoiding the excesses of this world, and being temperate in all things...

What better example do we have of temperance than our Savior, Jesus Christ?

אָמַץ amets: To be strong, to be courageous, to be firm, to strengthen
"[Ruth] was steadfastly minded to go with [Naomi]" Ruth 1:18
"Be...of a good courage, fear not." Deut 31:6
חיל chayil: strength, valour, army, wealth, capability
"thou art a virtuous woman." Ruth 3:11
"The Lord is with thee, thou mighty man of valour." Judges 6:12
Amets is like chayil. It's power for men and women through their different vehicles.
"The wicked flee when no man pursueth: but the righteous are bold as a lion." Proverbs 28:1
"Trust in the Lord with all thine heart; and lean not unto thine own understanding." Proverbs 3:5
I came across a commentary on Bible Hub that listed traits of righteousness: faith, integrity, generosity, truthful speech, and courage...
