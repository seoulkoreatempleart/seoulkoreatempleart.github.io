Scripture Study Reflections

Thoughts on Chistian discipleship as a covenant daughter of God.

אָמַץ amets : To be strong, to be courageous, to be firm, to strengthen
"[Ruth] was steadfastly minded to go with [Naomi]" Ruth 1:18
"Be...of a good courage, fear not." Deut 31:6
חיל chayil : strength, valour, army, wealth, capability
"thou art a virtuous woman." Ruth 3:11
"The Lord is with thee, thou mighty man of valour." Judges 6:12
Amets is like chayil. It's power for men and women through their different vehicles.
"The wicked flee when no man pursueth: but the righteous are bold as a lion." Proverbs 28:1
"Trust in the Lord with all thine heart; and lean not unto thine own understanding." Proverbs 3:5
I came across a commentary on Bible Hub that listed traits of righteousness: faith, integrity, generosity, truthful speech, and courage. I was really intrigued by the inclusion of 'courage.' I looked up the scripture referenced for 'courage,' which was Proverbs 28:1, "the righteous are bold as a lion." The word translated as 'bold,' or 'batach' in Hebrew, is translated as 'trust' elsewhere. For example, Proverbs 3:5, Trust in the Lord with all thine heart.

This is the commentary from Bible Hub on 'batach':

"The verb בָּטח expresses placing one’s full weight upon someone or something with calm assurance. Across the canon it always concerns an object of reliance—whether the covenant LORD, military strength, idols, political alliances, or oneself. The moral value of בָּטח is therefore determined by its object: when directed toward God it is faith, when toward anything else it is folly.

Approximately one-third of the occurrences appear in Psalms...

Because the covenant God commits Himself to His people, trusting Him becomes the appropriate human response. Lack of בָּטח toward the LORD is interpreted as covenant breach (Isaiah 30:1–3)."

Elder Dale G. Renlund says:

"When we consider the goodness of our Heavenly Father and Jesus Christ, our trust in Them increases. ...By acknowledging that every good thing comes from Jesus Christ...[w]e will have courage when confronted with seemingly impossible tasks and circumstances" (emphasis added, "Consider the Goodness and Greatness of God," Apr 2020 GC).

In the Psalms, David was often reflecting on God's goodness and mercy. Acknowledging that every good thing comes from Jesus Christ and considering the goodness of our Heavenly Father and Jesus Christ increases our ability to 'batach' (is that grammatically correct? I don't know), to be bold and trust in the Lord and Heavenly Father.

Seeing the definition for bold made my brain expand. I felt like I was seeing or understanding my true self for the first time. I had the beginning of an inkling of what it truly meant to be a daughter of God.

Being bold means have confidence because of my trust in the Lord. Being valiant comes as a consequence of putting my full weight on the Lord. He is the object of my reliance, not money or other people.
